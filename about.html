About the Game

Uncivilized is a free-to-play 4X strategy game set in the Ancient Era, currently in beta. Build cities, research technologies, raise armies, and forge an empire — but what makes Uncivilized different is how you deal with the civilizations around you.

The AI Negotiates Back

Every faction leader in Uncivilized is powered by AI that thinks, reasons, and responds to your words — not scripted dialogue trees, not pre-written responses. Tell the Warlord you'll defend his borders and he might believe you. Threaten the Oracle and she might call your bluff. Promise tribute to the Commander and break your word — he'll remember.

Six AI leaders, each with distinct personalities, motivations, and diplomatic styles. They form alliances, demand tribute, propose trades, declare wars, and make peace — all through natural conversation. Your words have consequences.

Diplomacy Changes Everything

In most strategy games, diplomacy is a menu you click through. In Uncivilized, it's the skill that determines whether your strategy works.

A brilliant military plan fails if every faction allies against you. A weak economy thrives if you negotiate the right trade deals. A science victory becomes possible when you convince two rivals to fight each other instead of you.

You can win by conquest, technology, culture, or outlasting everyone. But the path to any victory runs through negotiation.

Community-Driven

We plan to open-source the game engine after beta testing is complete. In the meantime, our beta testers are helping shape the game's direction through feedback, bug bounties, and feature requests.

The AI diplomacy system improves as more players interact with it. Every negotiation, every alliance, every betrayal helps the AI become a better diplomat.

Features

  • 60×40 hex map with tectonic plate terrain generation
  • 6 AI faction leaders with unique personalities
  • Natural language diplomacy — speak freely, the AI understands
  • AI reputation memory — leaders remember your behaviour and react accordingly
  • 25 technologies across the Ancient Era tech tree
  • 12 civics in a parallel culture tree
  • 19 buildings, 8 Wonders of the Ancient World
  • 4 governments: Chiefdom, Despotism, Republic, Oligarchy
  • 8 pantheons and religious foundations
  • 12 unit types with promotions and upgrade paths
  • Dynamic barbarian camps with specialist mercenaries
  • Trade routes, happiness, great people
  • 4 victory conditions: Domination, Science, Culture, Score
  • Global leaderboard and competitive tournaments
  • Vision system with explored/shroud/visible layers
  • Beta testing with bug bounties & rewards
